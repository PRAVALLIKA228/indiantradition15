Holi

Holi is a vibrant and joyous festival celebrated primarily in India and Nepal, marking the arrival of spring. Often referred to as the "Festival of Colors" or the "Festival of Love," it typically takes place in March.The festival has deep cultural and religious significance, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil. One of the most famous legends associated with Holi is the story of Holika and Prahlad, which represents the victory of devotion and faith.During Holi, people throw colored powders and water at each other, dance, sing, and enjoy festive foods.
